Legacy Project — EMF

Electromagnetic Field Simulation for Vectorized and Voxelized Reality

Fields are the truth. Structure gives them form. Artifacts preserve them forever. The EMF section is the canonical home for deterministic, GPU-first electromagnetic simulation inside The Bundle.

Mission

Physics-first, reproducible, performant.

Plug-and-play electromagnetic field simulation that ingests any 3D structure—molecular, mesoscopic, or macroscopic—and produces validated behavior with transparent provenance.

Architecture

Layered and deterministic.

Geometry, physics, solvers, runtime, and UI remain strictly separated. Solvers are stateless, pure, and GPU-oriented with explicit device and precision policies.

Artifact

The single source of truth.

Every run emits an immutable artifact containing structure, materials, solver configuration, environment, results, and validation diagnostics—always replayable and hashable.

Foundational Principles

Non-negotiable guardrails for the EMF system.

  1. Physics first—visualization follows correctness.
  2. Determinism is mandatory; every simulation can be replayed.
  3. GPU-class performance with explicit precision and stability policies.
  4. Artifacts over side effects; outputs are immutable and inspectable.
  5. Layered architecture with strict separation of concerns.
  6. No black boxes; all derived values trace back to equations and discretization.
  7. Longevity over fashion; frameworks may change, principles do not.
Simulation Modes

Choose the appropriate regime for the study.

  • Electrostatic / magnetostatic (Poisson-based)
  • Quasi-static EM
  • Time-domain full-wave (FDTD / Yee grid)
  • Frequency-domain harmonic analysis
  • Hybrid regimes (future milestone)

The chosen regime is explicit in every simulation artifact.

Boundary Conditions

Boundary behavior is part of the contract.

  • PEC / PMC
  • Absorbing boundaries (PML, ABC)
  • Periodic domains (crystalline / metamaterials)
  • Open-space approximations
Structural Input Model

Any 3D reality, vectorized or voxelized.

Vectorized Structures

Meshes, analytic primitives, molecular bonds, and atomic spheres.

Deterministic conversion to SDFs, voxel grids, or material property volumes.

Voxelized Structures

Density fields, permittivity/permeability maps, conductivity volumes.

Voxel grids carry coordinate frames, units, sampling resolution, and interpolation semantics.

Scale Bridging

Molecular → macroscopic.

Explicit unit discipline, coarse-grained material models, and resolution policies tuned to field gradients.

USD as Structural Backbone

What exists, not how it behaves.

Why USD

Hierarchical composition, layered overrides, time-sampled data, and industry adoption for structured scenes.

Responsibilities

Scene hierarchy, transforms, material region tagging, semantic grouping, and time-varying structure.

Not Responsibilities

USD never solves fields or replaces solver data structures—it defines structure and meaning only.

Validation & Integrity

Scientific honesty is mandatory.

Each solver is verified against analytical solutions, canonical benchmarks, and regression artifacts.

Execution is observable, interruptible, repeatable, and free from hidden global state.
